The Story

Formed in Mesa, Arizona in 1993, Jimmy Eat World emerged from the underground emo scene to become one of the most influential alternative rock bands of the 2000s. With their breakthrough album "Bleed American" (later renamed "Jimmy Eat World"), they brought emo into the mainstream while never losing their authentic edge.

Led by Jim Adkins' distinctive vocals and the band's masterful songwriting, they've released 10 studio albums and continue to tour worldwide, inspiring new generations of fans with their honest, emotional rock anthems.
